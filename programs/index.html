Primary Program

Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP)

Our flagship program. Direct payment to a landlord or housing provider to cover rent in arrears or other imminent housing costs, preventing an active or threatened eviction.

How it works:

  • Household submits an application with documentation (lease, notice, income, hardship explanation)
  • Board reviews the application at its next meeting
  • If approved, payment is sent directly to the landlord or property manager
  • Funds are never paid to the applicant in cash

Typical assistance range: Generally between $300 and $9,000 per household, depending on documented need and available funds.

Secondary Tool

Zero-Interest Forgivable Loans

For working families with a clear path to recovery, assistance may be provided as a zero-interest forgivable loan rather than a grant. This allows donor funds to be recycled to help additional families over time.

Key terms:

  • No interest charged
  • No monthly payment required during the hardship period
  • Loans may be forgiven entirely based on documented continued hardship or successful completion of the stability plan
  • No penalties for borrowers who cannot repay

We do not report loans to credit bureaus. We do not pursue collection on hardship-driven loans. The goal of the program is to keep families housed — not to create new financial burdens.

What we don't do

To stay focused on our mission and use donor funds responsibly, HOUSE, Inc. does not currently provide:

  • Direct cash payments to individuals
  • Mortgage payoffs (we may assist with mortgage arrears to prevent foreclosure in narrow cases)
  • Security deposits for new leases (occasionally possible, case-by-case)
  • Utility-only assistance (we focus on rent and core housing costs)
  • Ongoing monthly rent subsidies

How decisions are made

Every application is reviewed by our volunteer board of directors. The board considers:

  • Documented imminence of eviction or housing loss
  • Income qualification and household size
  • Prior rental history and landlord communication
  • The household's plan for ongoing stability
  • Available funds and the impact on our ability to help other applicants

Decisions are communicated in writing. We do not guarantee approval of any application.
