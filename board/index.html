Board & Governance

HOUSE, Inc. is governed by a volunteer board of directors.

About our board

HOUSE, Inc. is led by a board of directors composed of individuals with professional backgrounds in medicine, real estate, education, and small business. Board members serve without compensation.

The board is responsible for:

  • Reviewing and deciding on every assistance application
  • Setting program policy, eligibility, and assistance limits
  • Approving the annual budget and financial statements
  • Ensuring compliance with federal and state nonprofit requirements
  • Stewarding donor relationships and organizational fundraising

Current board roster

A complete and current list of board members is maintained in our governance records and is available on written request. To request a current roster with names, roles, and bios, email info@houseinc501c3.com with the subject line "Board Roster Request."

We are intentional about publishing accurate, current information. Because board composition can change between updates, the public-facing roster is provided directly by the organization rather than maintained as a static page.

Governance practices

  • Board members serve staggered terms
  • The board meets at least three times per year, with additional meetings as needed for application review
  • Board members are expected to participate in fundraising and outreach
  • Conflict-of-interest disclosures are reviewed annually
  • All assistance applications over defined thresholds require a documented board vote

Joining the board

HOUSE, Inc. periodically recruits new board members. We look for individuals who:

  • Are aligned with our mission of preventing homelessness
  • Bring professional expertise that strengthens the board (e.g., nonprofit finance, real estate, law, social work, fundraising, marketing)
  • Have the time to commit to quarterly meetings, application review, and at least one fundraising or outreach activity per year
  • Are willing to make a personal financial contribution at a level meaningful to them

If you are interested in being considered for a future opening, please email info@houseinc501c3.com with a brief introduction and a description of the perspective you would bring to the board.

Contacting the board

For governance-related correspondence, including board nominations, conflict-of-interest questions, or formal board communications, email info@houseinc501c3.com with the subject line Board Governance.
